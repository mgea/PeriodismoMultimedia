Web design concepts

Web design

Web design refers to the design of websites and user experience. It focuses on aspects of website development rather than software development. A web designer works on a website's appearance, layout and content

learn more

Information Architecture

Information architecture is about helping people understand their surroundings and find what they’re looking for, in the real world as well as online. IA focuses on organizing, structuring, and labeling content in an effective and sustainable way. learn more

Layout

Layout design is the process of arranging visual elements like text, images, and shapes—on a given page. Elements are arranged to guide the viewer's eye and emphasize important information using a coherent hierarchy

learn more

Sitemap

A sitemap is a blueprint of your website, a hierarchical diagram of a website or application, that shows how pages are prioritized, linked, and labeled.

learn more

Responsive Web Design

Approach to designing web content that appears regardless of the resolution governed by the device using viewport breakpoints to adjust logically on tablets, phones, and desktops of any resolution.

learn more

Logo

An essential element of any brand, a logo is also a standard part of a website. A logo represents the company and makes it recognizable and memorable to both current and potential customers.

learn more

Breadcrumb

A breadcrumbis a type of secondary navigation scheme that reveals the user’s location in a website or Web application, allowing users to find their way around and improvinng the findability*of website sections and pages.

learn more

CTA

CTA (Call-to-Action) is a main element in a web page that solicit an action from the user. It is a clickable buttons that when clicked, perform an action, focusing the user in the main goal

learn more

Search bar

A search bar allows users to enter keywords or queries to search for specific content within the site. It may contains advanced search option (filters, auto-suggestions)

learn more

Sidebar

A sidebar is a secondary navigation of your site but less critical than the information in your header. It is used for secondary webpages, related information or just Ads.

learn more

Blog

Blogs are groupings of articles or posts on a variety of topics related to your site-goal showwed in a reverse chronological order. It is dynamic content and updated frequently

learn more

Forms

The forms is used to to collect user inputs sent to a server for processing. There are many standard form: login, print, purchase, personal data

learn more

Carousels

Slideshow of photo or video content on a website, dynamic (displaying with motion speed) or static (navigated manually with arrow buttons)

learn more

Footer

The footer is the bottom section of a webpage that contains important information and links such as copyright notices, contact details, social networks, privacy policies and additional navigation options.

Page Style Layout

Hero section

A hero section is the visible area under your logo and menu, including information about what you have to offer, benefits and what action you should take next.

learn more

Landing Page

A landing page is a standalone webpage created to support a specific marketing campaign, encourage users to complete a specific call to action (purchase, sing in..). Based on hero section

learn more

One-Page design

All of the site content is on a single web page. Navigation is done through scrolling, often with shortcuts to jump to particular sections and sometimes with parallax scrolling effects.

learn more

Card Design

Use content blocks that resemble physical cards of various shapes and sizes. It may be simetrical cards (with equal dimensions on a grid) or fluid layout (with different size).

Magazine Layout

Focusing on featured article, secondary articles and multiple columns for content divided into sections. Great for sites with a great deal of content added daily.
